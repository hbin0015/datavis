Australia's Tourism Comeback

Australia's visitor economy is moving again, but it has not come back in one neat way. This page follows the recovery after border closures, then looks at airports, seasons, spending, and the countries visitors are coming from.

1

The return was slow, then suddenly normal again

The first view sets up the main story: tourism did not bounce back in one clean line. It climbed slowly, with seasonal jumps and dips, before getting close to the 2019 level.

Visitor arrivals compared with the 2019 level

The dashed line marks the pre-pandemic benchmark. The orange line shows how far the recovery had reached.

Arrivals and departures moved together again

Outbound travel also recovered strongly, so the tourism comeback is not only about people entering Australia, but also Australians travelling again.

2

Recovery does not mean every traveller group behaves the same

Domestic overnight trips bring the largest spend, while international travellers are smaller in trip count but still important because of how much money they bring into Australia.

Spend by traveller type

Use the slider to hide lower-growth categories and focus on stronger spending growth.

Trips compared with spending

This shows that more trips does not always mean proportionally more spend.

3

Seasonality still shapes tourism

The heatmap makes the seasonal pattern easier to see than a long table. Darker months show stronger visitor numbers.

Monthly visitor seasonality

Change the year to compare the most recent available months.

4

The comeback is concentrated through big gateways

Most international movement happens through a small number of airport gateways. The map is not just decoration: it shows the spatial concentration of Australia's tourism entry points.

Major airport gateways

Bubble size represents total passengers. The slider can hide smaller airports to reduce clutter.

Domestic and international airport traffic

This range-dot chart compares the domestic and international mix for each airport.

5

Source markets are changing, not just recovering

The final section shifts from overall recovery to the people behind it. Some markets are near or above 2019, while others are still rebuilding.

Top source countries, March 2019 vs March 2026

Grouped bars make the before-and-after change easy for a general Australian audience to read.

Where visitors planned to stay

Tick the box to switch from raw arrivals to year-on-year growth.

Victoria's visitor mix

Because this assignment is made from Victoria, this compact stacked bar gives the local view of source countries.

6

So, is tourism actually “back”?

The last two charts are here to make the ending less vague. Some source countries are already above their 2019 level, but the state-level picture is still uneven.

Which source countries recovered the most?

The dotted line is the old 2019 level. Anything to the right has passed it.

State stays: March 2019 vs March 2026

This slope chart compares where visitors planned to stay before and after the recovery.
